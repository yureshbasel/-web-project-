Chicken

Chicken is a widely consumed meat known for its tender texture and mild flavor, making it a versatile ingredient in various cuisines. It is a lean source of protein and is commonly prepared through grilling, roasting, frying, or simmering in soups and curries. Chicken pairs well with numerous herbs and spices, making it a favorite in dishes such as roasted chicken, stir-fries, and chicken biryani.

Beef

Beef, sourced from cattle, is known for its hearty and bold flavor, making it a staple in many global cuisines. It is highly versatile, being used in dishes like steaks, burgers, pot roasts, and soups. Different cuts of beef, from tenderloin to brisket, offer varied textures and flavors, making it suitable for a range of cooking styles, from grilling to slow braising.

Mutton

Mutton, typically derived from sheep, has a rich and gamey flavor that appeals to those who enjoy more robust meats. It is often used in traditional dishes such as curries, stews, and biryanis. The texture of mutton can be tender or slightly chewy, depending on the cut and cooking method. Slow cooking or marination is often employed to enhance its taste and tenderness.
